Our Journey

A Glimpse of Our Story

Our love story begun through our service in the church. I had joined the Edify church to keep fellowship while I pursued graduate medicine in Korlebu Teaching Hospital. Christopher served as the associate pastor in the same church. We were assigned to the same hostel for evangelism. Looking back perhaps the closing of doors in our faces and cold reception brought us together to encourage each other. We worked on many other projects together : worship nights, church hangouts for several years. Our relationship remained platonic for several years. I was single and he was in a relationship then. On one such evening, I inquired about how his relationship life was going because I had been expecting a wedding invitation for a while. He confided in me how it had come to an end. I left that meeting with a resolve to pray for him that God would send him a good woman. There was a holy anger in my spirit as to how someone who had loved God and served so faithfully lacked a help mate. I still can't believe I was praying for my own self. After that conversation Pastor Chris begun to check up a bit more frequently. He suddenly needed to keep the camera bag because it suddenly became heavy. Lol. The awkward church side hugs became full hugs, the conversation moved from "have you bought the communion" to "have you eaten". One evening walking to prayer meeting I prayed slightly in my heart that God would bring a Godly man my way. That same evening Lydia my close friend called to ask if I had seen anyone in the church I was considering. I mentioned that I saw Pastor Chris as my brother. She asked which of my mother's children he was 😅. I begun to think about him a bit more and started a fast for some clarity. Shortly afterwards he asked me to go out for dinner with him after rehearsal. At that date we had a long conversation and had to leave because the restaurant was closing. I knew at that meeting he would be my husband. There was so much peace just considering the idea. I told my mum I felt too young to date. She called me a foolish girl and encouraged me to go ahead. As the days went by the awkward side hugs in church became full hugs. The short official calls for church became long night calls. Time flies when you're having fun. Today, we celebrate a year and a half of growing in love and understanding (no drama here) trusting God for many more years of bliss. 🥰❤️
